About Me

A human-centered Data Scientist and Software Engineer actively pursuing a part time masters degree in analytics on the Data Science track from the Georiga Institude of Technology. I’ve been programming since a young age, and it’s always been my dream to contribute to an organization where I can make a meaningful impact. My bachelor's degree in Computer Science with a concentration in Big Data from the University of Wyoming equipped me with the skills to turn that dream into a reality. Now, I’m looking for a full-time opportunity where I can apply my talents to positively impact others.

For the past four years I've primarily used what I've learned to participate in A.I. focused research and student mentorship. I was lucky enough to be able to contribute to the University of Wyoming's Artificial Intelligence Lab where I helped a graduate student and mentor of mine implement A.I. guided pathfinding algorithms into robots and establishing a robotics club. We used a combination of Q-Learning and convolutional neural networks to better improve autonomous movement through image processing. These robots were also instrumental in launching a robotics club aimed at educating students about real-world applications of programming.

During that time, I also worked as a teaching assistant for introductory Computer Science and a Peer Mentor Analyst for the Student Success team. During my time with the Student Success team, I mentored students and did research on factors that contribute most to student retention rates. This role enhanced my understanding of data-driven decision-making. Most of my time on our analysis team was spent cleaning, visualizing, and analyzing data in order to figure out what actions our team could take that would help student graduate. With the combined effort of our team, we were able to see a 10% improvement in student retention rates. This experience also led to my selection as an advisor on a board of professors focused on organizing and implementing innovative first-year courses centered on success strategies and resource management.

Outside of my passion for computer science and analytics, I love to spend my weekends hiking through the rocky mountains of Colorado during the summers. When I can, I like to put time into my passion for photography, and I'm currently shooting on a DSLR Cannon EOS Rebel T6. And during the winters, I spend most of my time skiing and playing video games. I also play guitar whenever I get a chance and hope to one day finish an album I've been working on with my Dad. I've been listening to a lot of Beck recently, specifically Sea Change and Morning Phase, so if I had to recommend anything at the moment, it would be these two albums.
